1. Part 1 Motional EMF

Parts to use:

  • Horse-shoe magnet (strong B-Field)
  • 2 meters of copper wire

  • Ammeter with 2 banana connectors

    Challenges:

    1. Measure the field direction with a compass
    2. Create the largest induced current you can make with a single loop
    3. Use Lenz’s law to predict the direction of induced current
    4. Create the largest induced current you can make with as many loops as you wish
    5. Create a coil of two or more loops which does not display any effects of induction

2. Part 2: Transformer

Parts to use:

  • AC-DC power supply
  • 1 meter of copper wire (primary)
  • 2 meter of copper wire (secondary)
  • 2 Ammeters with probes
  • Steel cylindrical mass

  • Aluminum cylindrical mass

    Challenges:

    1. Set up an ammeter to measure the current supplied from the power supply
    2. Wrap the wire around each metal chunk and see which creates a stronger magnet using DC current
    3. Create a transformer using both wires (pay attention to number of wraps)
    4. Investigate which type of current (DC or AC) is used in transformers

3. Part 3: Eddy Currents

Parts to use:

  • Copper tube with magnets
  • stop watch
  • meter stick
  • digital scale

Challenges:

  1. Find the force exerted on the magnet as it falls
  2. Use a FBD to calculate the force required to produce the observed motion
  3. Contrast this with air drag in free fall
