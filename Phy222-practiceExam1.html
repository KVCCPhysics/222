Problem 1:

Two charges are configured as shown below. A \(5 ~ \mu C\) charge is in the bottom left and a \(+3 ~ \mu C\) in the top right separated by distances \(x=1.5 ~ m\) and \(y=1.0 ~ m\).

  1. Calculate the Force (vector) felt on each charge. I encourage the use of unit vectors, but you may use right angle trig if you prefer.
  2. Calculate the Electric Field at point \(\mathbb{P}\)
  3. Calculate the electric Potential at point \(\mathbb{P}\)
  4. Sketch the electric field and equipotential contours of the two charge system

Problem 2:

Four charges are configured in a rectangle with sides \(x=0.5 ~ m\) and \(y=0.2m\). The corners of the rectangle has charge \(q_1=+1 ~ \mu C\), \(q_2=-2 ~ \mu C\), \(q_3=+3 ~ \mu C\) and \(q_4=-4 ~ \mu C\).

  1. Calculate the Force felt on the top right charge.
  2. Calculate the Electric Field at the center of the rectangle (point P)
  3. Calculate the Electric Potential at the center of the rectangle (point P)
  4. Sketch the electric field and equipotential contours of the 4 charge system

Problem 3:

Three charges are configured in an equilateral triangle. The charges are all equal and positive.

  1. Calculate the electric field at points A, B, C, and D.
  2. Calculate the electric potential at points A, B, C and D.
  3. Sketch the electric potential and electric field of the 3 charge system.

Problem 4:

A uniform plane of charge is fixed in place and extends to the horizon. Above the plane, a charge \(q=100\mu C\) and mass of \(m=0.27 ~ kg\) is initially at rest, but free to move in any direction.

  1. Draw the normal vector to the plane of charge and define your coordinate system
  2. Use Gauss’ Law to derive the electric field for planar symmetry
  3. Integrate the electric field to find the electric potential as a function of distance above the plane.
  4. Find the velocity of the stationary charge q, after the charge travels \(2 ~ m\) starting from rest.

Problem 5:

An thin wire of length \(\ell=3.5 ~ m\) and charge \(q = -70 ~ /mu C\) is at the center of a cylindrical metal tube with charge \(Q=140 ~ \mu C\).

  1. Draw the normal vector to the outside cylinder and define your coordinate system
  2. Use Gauss’ Law to derive the electric field for cylindrical symmetry
  3. Integrate the electric field to find the electric potential as a function of distance away from the the cylinder.
  4. Find the charge distribution on the inside and outside of the conducting shell

Problem 6:

A small charge \(q = -50 ~ /mu C\) is at the center of a spherical metal shell with charge \(Q=+20 ~ \mu C\).

  1. Draw the normal vector to the outside sphere and define your coordinate system
  2. Use Gauss’ Law to derive the electric field for spherical symmetry
  3. Integrate the electric field to find the electric potential as a function of distance away from the the sphere.
  4. Find the charge distribution on the inside and outside of the conducting shell
